## ğŸ—“ï¸ Sprint Planning
**Objective:** Definition of the Sprint Goal and the Sprint Backlog.
*Goal: Align on the "What" and the "How" for the next 2 weeks.*

**What to Bring:**
* **Product Owner:** A prioritized Product Backlog with "Ready" stories at the top.
* **Scrum Master:** Historical velocity data and team capacity (holidays, training).
* **The Team:** Understanding of the current architecture and technical constraints. Technical specs for any **Lambda/Infrastructure** changes.
* **Tools:**
    * Digital board (Jira/Azure DevOps) or physical sticky notes.
    * Estimation tools (Planning Poker cards or digital app).
    * Calendar to visualize the Sprint duration and bank holidays.
* **How to Prepare:**
    - [ ] **PO:** Ensure top items have clear Acceptance Criteria (AC).
    - [ ] **Devs:** Review the top 5-10 tasks *before* the session to identify technical risks.
    - [ ] **Team:** Check the **Power BI** capacity dashboard for upcoming holidays/leave.

## â˜€ï¸ Daily Scrum
**Objective:** Inspect progress toward the Sprint Goal and adapt the plan.
*Goal: Sync on progress and surface blockers.*

**What to Bring:**
* **The Team:** Updates on their progress and knowledge of any Specific blockers (e.g., "Waiting for Brand assets" or "Lambda timeout error").
* **Scrum Master:** Observation of patterns (is the team talking to each other or just reporting status?).
* **Tools:**
    * **The Sprint Board:** Visualized (screen share or physical board).
    * **The Burndown Chart:** To see if the team is on track mid-sprint.
    * **Timer:** To ensure the event stays strictly under 15 minutes.
* **How to Prepare:**
    - [ ] Update your task status **before** the meeting starts.
    - [ ] Identify if your work impacts the **Mobile/Web redesign** sync.
    - [ ] "Walk the board" from right (Done) to left (To-Do).

## ğŸ” Sprint Review
**Objective:** Inspect the Increment and adapt the Product Backlog.
*Goal: Inspect the increment and gather stakeholder feedback.*

**What to Bring:**
* **The Team:** A "Done" Increment that is actually working (no PowerPoint demos of half-finished code).
* **Product Owner:** Invites for the right stakeholders and a vision of the next steps. Key metrics (e.g., "New automation test coverage achieved").
* **Stakeholders:** Feedback, market insights, and business requirements.
* **Tools:**
    * Environment where the product can be demonstrated live.A working demo (Staging environment).
    * Access to the Product Backlog to make real-time adjustments based on feedback.
* **How to Prepare:**
    - [ ] Verify that the **Lambda** functions are warm and the demo data is ready.
    - [ ] Prepare a 2-minute "Brand Spotlight" for any newly onboarded partners.
    - [ ] Ensure the **Mobile app** build is installed on your test device.

## ğŸ”„ Sprint Retrospective
**Objective:** Plan ways to increase quality and effectiveness.


**What to Bring:**

* **Tools:**
    * **Facilitation Format:** (e.g., Start/Stop/Continue, Sailboat, or 4Ls).
    * **Whiteboard:** (Miro, Mural, or physical) for capturing action items.
    * **Previous Action Items:** To check if the team actually improved from the last session.
    - An open mind and "Blameless" attitude.
* **How to Prepare:**
    - [ ] Reflect on the **Working Agreement**â€”did we follow our commitments?
    - [ ] Identify one "Process Improvement" for the next sprint.
    - [ ] Review any "Redesign Phase" friction points.
